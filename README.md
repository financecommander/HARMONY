# Dubau Yemeni Coffee
### Concept Overview & Operating Model

Dubau Yemeni Coffee is a late-night specialty coffee concept inspired by traditional Yemeni coffee houses and adapted for dense urban environments in the Northeastern United States. The brand focuses on premium Yemeni coffee, social atmosphere, and extended hours that capture demand after traditional cafés close.

The model emphasizes **small footprint cafés**, **high-margin beverages**, **centralized food production**, and **late-night operations** to maximize revenue per square foot.

---

# Vision

To build a recognizable regional Yemeni coffee brand that combines:

- Authentic Yemeni coffee traditions  
- Modern café design and hospitality  
- Late-night community gathering spaces  
- Highly efficient centralized production  

The goal is to scale a **cluster-based café network** throughout Rhode Island and Massachusetts.

---

# Core Strategy

## 1. Build Our Own Brand Instead of Franchising

Multiple franchise models were explored, including national and regional coffee concepts. After evaluating costs, brand restrictions, and operational limitations, the decision was made to create an **independent concept**.

Reasons include:

- Franchise royalties reduce already tight café margins
- Limited ability to adapt menu and hours
- Lack of differentiation in saturated coffee markets
- Inability to leverage centralized kitchen assets

Creating a proprietary brand allows full control over:

- Menu innovation
- Brand identity
- Operating hours
- Cost structure
- Expansion strategy

---

# Target Operating Model

## Small Footprint Cafés

Typical location size:

**500 – 1,200 sq ft**

Key advantages:

- Lower rent
- Faster buildout
- Faster break-even
- Easier staffing model
- Works well in dense urban environments

Most locations focus on **high-margin beverage sales with limited onsite food preparation**.

---

# Late Night Coffeehouse Model

Traditional cafés close early (3–6 PM). Dubau Yemeni Coffee focuses on a different demand curve.

Typical operating hours:

```

Morning:   8 AM – 12 PM
Afternoon: 12 PM – 6 PM
Evening:   6 PM – 10 PM
Late Night: 10 PM – 2 AM (core social period)

```

Late-night traffic includes:

- Students
- Hospitality workers
- Young professionals
- Social gatherings
- Dessert and coffee customers
- Post-dinner crowd

Yemeni coffee houses historically operate late into the evening, making this culturally aligned with the concept.

# Buildout Philosophy

Design should balance **traditional Yemeni inspiration with modern café aesthetics**.

Elements include:

- warm lighting
- textured plaster or stone finishes
- brass accents
- patterned tile
- communal seating
- lounge-style seating areas
- visually distinctive coffee preparation area

# Revenue Model

Primary revenue sources:

1. Specialty coffee drinks
2. Tea and chai beverages
3. Dessert and pastry sales
4. Late-night social traffic
5. Event or group gatherings

# Cluster Expansion Strategy

- central kitchen
- distribution logistics

- brand marketing

Southeastern Massachusetts
